Apremavi  |  Brazil

“You are telling me we can gather real-time data on illegal deforestation in the rainforest without endangering our staff....WOW!”


Client

Apremavi is an environmental NGO based in Brazil and is safeguarding and restoring the Upper Itajaí Valley area of the South American Atlantic rainforest.

Objectives

To precisely map and quantify the advance or retreat of the forest in the Upper Itajal Valley. Lack of government funding means the client is unable to accurately report on their main area of concern.





Quickbird used UAVs for taking aerial images and employed state-of-the-art GIS technology to:

  • Produce in-depth reports on forest locales to enable better understanding of problems when compared to traditional satelite imagery,
  • Generate high-resoultion land use maps in the region to be used for spatial analysis and planning,
  • Recommend best land management practices based on the data gathered,
  • Provide insights and analysis reports on the fluid state of illegal deforestation, agricultural land use, urban development and forest cover

Duro Felguera  |  Spain

“It saved us a ton of time and money that we have otherwise spent on them laser scanners.”


Client

Duro Felguera

Objectives

To estimate the volume of coal extracted out a mine


The stereo images captured by Quickbird drones were postprocessed and pressed through a photogrammetry software to calculate heights and estimate coal volume. The correction between points in each pair of stereo images was done manually to reduce error.

  • A very-high resolution(5 cm) spatial model was generated
  • Our optically calibrated cameras ensured high fidelity data capture
  • Manual correction was applied to shadows/building during post-processing to deliver an accurate dataset
  • Deliverables, including point cloud and digital elevation models, could easily be used with industry-standard GIS software such as ArcMap/ArcGIS, AutoCAD and 3DSMax



Gaia  |  Spain

“Aerial scans of the forest helped us identify issues faster and optimize data collection.”


Client

Gaia

Objectives

To track, geolocate and tag health & safety issues on trekking trails


Quickbird UAVs survey the forest, evaluate the quality of the environment surrounding the trail and estimate the severity of the problem. The drone imagery advised the ground-level data collection following the scans and a database was generated to organize teamwork and visualize all the findings.
